## Reflection

Teaching at Rockhurst felt like a natural next step, even if it wasn’t the path I originally expected. While I enjoyed my career in the technology industry, I realized my true passion has always been people. I kept finding myself drawn to spaces that were adjacent to education: serving on curriculum committees, working as a TA, mentoring with organizations like Girls Who Code, and leading the Kansas City Spartans alumni group. Over time, it became clear how much I valued and was energized by being in the classroom, and Rockhurst became the clear choice.

As an alumna of Blue Springs South High School, I came to Rockhurst with a fairly neutral perspective, mostly shaped by its reputation for strong athletics. Over the past two years, I’ve come to see something much deeper. Rockhurst is truly a place of cura personalis, where we care for the whole student: academically, athletically, and personally. Students are encouraged to explore multiple interests, and it’s common to see them deeply involved across academics, co-curriculars, service, athletics, and leadership. There is a strong sense of belonging, an “us” mentality, that comes from students contributing so much to the life of the school and from faculty and staff who genuinely know and support them. I’ve been humbled by the trust students place in us and challenged every day to become a better educator because of them.

Teaching Computer Science, Cybersecurity, and coaching Esports has been unexpectedly fun. Our students are creative, motivated, and incredibly talented. It has been exciting to introduce new courses like Cybersecurity Fundamentals and Computer Hardware & Operating Systems, while also connecting classroom learning to the broader technology industry through competitions, certification prep, labs, and company site visits. I’ve also enjoyed inviting friends and Rockhurst alumni to share their professional experiences and show students the many paths available in technology.

Esports has been an especially rewarding surprise. It has been meaningful to combine my personal interest in gaming with my background as a student-athlete. The program has created another opportunity for students to challenge themselves, build community, and even pursue college scholarships. Bringing home some hardware in our first season was exciting, but the real success is seeing students grow in confidence, belonging, and leadership.

Looking ahead, I hope these programs continue to help students practice discernment and discover what excites them. Whether they find a passion in computer science or realize another path is a better fit, both outcomes are valuable. I also hope to grow the Esports program to include more teams and support staff roles in broadcasting, graphic design, and social media, creating meaningful experiential learning opportunities beyond gameplay. My goal is to keep creating opportunities for students to grow, take risks, and leave Rockhurst knowing they were truly seen and supported.

- Gabby Kelsch, Computer Science Teacher and Esports Coach
